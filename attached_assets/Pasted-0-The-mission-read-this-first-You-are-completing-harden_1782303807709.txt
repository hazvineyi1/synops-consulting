0. The mission (read this first)

You are completing, hardening, and shipping a product that already contains its own differentiator — and is currently hiding it in the wrong place.

The intelligence of this entire system lives in curriculumRules.ts: a deterministic engine that reads an objective's verb to detect its Bloom's level, flags vague or unmeasurable outcomes, checks standards alignment, scores assessment coverage, and produces a weighted 5-category QA report with a Bloom distribution. It is real, it is good, and right now it runs client-side, inside the free marketing demo — where it generates leads but never persists into a paying customer's project.

Meanwhile the paid product's QA stage is dumb CRUD: it stores whatever pass/fail counts and findings a human types. The objectives table has no Bloom column. qa_checks is a single oedi_rubric dimension. designMethod is free text. The product a customer buys today is a curriculum-flavored Trello with a standards table — not the engine they saw in the demo.

Your job is to correct that inversion and turn this repository into a self-serve, profitable, production-grade SaaS: move the magic behind the paywall, point the whole product at one buyer who pays every year (nursing and health-professions programs facing accreditation site visits and self-studies), instrument everything so the founder can see what is working and what is not, and make it genuinely shippable.

The day a nursing program director can paste their objectives, watch the engine flag the unmeasurable ones, and export a CCNE-aligned evidence packet without anyone on a sales call — this stops being consulting with a nice login and becomes a product. Build toward that moment.


1. Guardrails — preserve, do not regress

The engineering foundation is an asset. Protect it.


Keep the contract-first discipline. OpenAPI is the source of truth → regenerate the typed hooks and Zod schemas after every schema change. Do not hand-write types that should be generated.
The server stays the security boundary. All validation, authorization, and tenant scoping happen server-side. The client is never trusted.
Do not weaken multi-tenant isolation or the audited impersonation safety. Keep the existing tenant-isolation and impersonation tests green; add to them, never delete them.
Keep the 4-stage pipeline (Intake → Design → QA → Handoff) as the container — but re-center it on outcomes → assessments → alignment (see Phase 1).
Keep the standards crosswalk model (objective/assessment ↔ competency). This is the accreditation gold; everything in Phase 2 builds on it.


Do NOT expand into these this cycle (they are the platform dream, not the wedge): LMS integrations (Canvas/Blackboard/D2L), program-level PLO mapping, a multi-accreditor library, an SME portal, a multi-framework theory navigator. Stub or hide any half-built versions; do not invest in them now.

Demote from the customer-facing product (keep the working code, but move it to an internal/admin-only area — it runs the services business, it is not what a buyer pays recurring dollars for): meeting recordings + AI transcription, the agendas engine, builder allocation, class rosters/memberships, time tracking, the ledger, the school report. Retire the Adaptive Reading demo from the public site entirely — one demo, one message.


2. Phase 1 — Promote the engine into the paid product (highest leverage)

This is the single most valuable change in the codebase. Everything else depends on it.


Schema + contract changes.

Add cognitiveLevel (Bloom's level enum) and measurabilityStatus (e.g. measurable | vague | unmeasurable) to the objectives table.
Replace the single-dimension qa_checks.oedi_rubric with a structured report (jsonb) column that stores the full 5-category weighted QA result, the Bloom distribution, and per-finding detail.
Migrate existing data safely (no destructive drops without a backfill path). Add DB migrations; do not mutate the schema by hand in production.
Regenerate the OpenAPI client, hooks, and Zod schemas.



Lift the engine out of the demo and into a shared library.

Move the pure functions of curriculumRules.ts (evaluateCourse and its helpers) into a shared lib package callable by both the public demo and the API. One engine, two callers — no duplicated logic.
Expose POST /compass/projects/:id/qa/evaluate that runs the engine against the project's real persisted data and writes the structured report into qa_checks.



Wire it into the real product UX.

Design Workspace: as objectives are written, give live measurability + Bloom feedback inline ("this objective isn't measurable," "you're at 80% recall-level verbs," "this assessment is orphaned"). The same feedback the demo gives — but on the customer's actual course.
QA stage: replace the manual pass/fail form with a real, engine-generated, scored 5-category report and Bloom distribution. A human can still annotate, but the score is computed, not typed.





Phase 1 done when: a real project produces a persisted, scored QA report from real objectives, and the Design Workspace shows live feedback as objectives are authored.


3. Phase 2 — One-click accreditation evidence export

This is the artifact that replaces months of spreadsheet work and justifies the price.


Turn the crosswalk + the persisted QA report into a formatted alignment matrix / evidence packet mapped to one accreditor's standards. Start with CCNE (it plays to the founder's nursing / managed-care depth). Build the export so a second accreditor (e.g. ABET) can be added later without rework, but ship one fully first.
Export to PDF and .docx, branded per tenant (ties into Phase 4 white-label).
The packet should be self-study-ready: standards on one axis, objectives/assessments on the other, with the QA findings and Bloom evidence attached.


Phase 2 done when: a user can generate a CCNE-aligned, tenant-branded evidence packet from a real project in one click and download it as PDF and Word.


4. Phase 3 — Make it possible to want to pay (monetization)

There is currently no monetization surface at all — no plans, no value metric, no checkout, no trial path. Build one.


Pricing page anchored against the buyer's real pain (accreditation consultants at $300–500/hr; months of self-study prep). Make the value obvious.
Value metric that grows with value: meter on active courses (or programs under accreditation) — pick active courses for v1. Do not meter on seats alone.
Plans/tiers with clear feature gating. White-label and multi-accreditor exports belong in higher tiers (see Phase 4).
Stripe billing: checkout, subscription management, upgrade/downgrade, cancellation, and a billing portal. Handle webhooks server-side.
Product-led trial path: a low-friction "start a course, see your QA score in 10 minutes" flow that does not require a sales call. Closed/hand-provisioned registration can remain an option for enterprise, but a self-serve trial must exist and convert to paid.
Trial → paid funnel must be measurable end to end (see Phase 5).


Phase 3 done when: a brand-new user can sign up, start a trial, create a course, run QA, hit a gated feature, upgrade via Stripe, and land on a paid plan — with no human in the loop.


5. Phase 4 — White-label options (sellable, configurable)

Per-tenant branding as a paid capability — not the product's focus, but a real upsell.


Per-tenant logo, color palette/theme, product name, and email sender identity.
Optional custom domain support (gate behind the top tier; safe defaults if unset).
White-label settings live in tenant admin and apply consistently across the app, the evidence-packet exports, and transactional emails.
Ship sane defaults so a non-white-label tenant looks polished out of the box.


Phase 4 done when: a tenant can upload branding and see it render consistently across the product UI and the exported evidence packet.


6. Phase 5 — Telemetry, audit, and "what's working / what's not"

Instrument the product so the founder can see, per-tenant and globally, who is doing what, when, from where, for how long — and where they succeed or drop off. Separate the two concerns cleanly:

A. Security & audit logging (accountability)

Capture an immutable audit/access log for sensitive and authentication events:


Who (user + tenant), when (timestamp), from where (IP address, user-agent/device), what (action), and session duration.
Log: signup, login/logout, password/credential changes, role changes, impersonation start/stop (extend the existing audited impersonation), billing changes, data export, and tenant-admin actions.
Store securely (encrypted at rest), role-gate access to the logs, and set a retention policy.


B. Product analytics (iteration)

Capture a structured event stream with a clear taxonomy so engagement and funnels are answerable:


Events: auth.signup, auth.login, course.create, objective.create, objective.edit, qa.evaluate.run (record computed score, Bloom distribution, flag count, run duration), export.generate (accreditor, format), billing.trial_start, billing.checkout_start, billing.subscribe, billing.cancel, session.start / session.end (with duration), and feature.view (route + dwell time).
Founder analytics console (extend the existing platform console) with, per-tenant and global:

Activation funnel: signup → first course → first QA run → first export → paid.
Engagement: DAU/WAU, session length, time-on-task per pipeline stage (Intake/Design/QA/Handoff).
QA outcomes: average QA score, % of objectives flagged unmeasurable, score improvement over time per course.
Revenue: MRR, trial→paid conversion, churn.
Health / "what's not working": error rates, slow endpoints, and drop-off points (where users abandon the funnel or a stage).



Make "what's working vs. what's not" a literal dashboard view: top adopted features and highest-completion flows on one side; highest drop-off, highest error, and longest-time-on-task friction points on the other.


C. Privacy (do not skip — IP and behavior tracking triggers obligations)


A privacy policy page and a consent mechanism at signup covering analytics and IP logging.
Data minimization + retention limits; prefer hashing/truncating IPs where full IPs are not required.
Data export and deletion endpoints for GDPR/CCPA-style requests.
All analytics/audit access is role-gated and encrypted at rest.


Phase 5 done when: the founder console answers "who used what, when, from where, for how long, and where they dropped off" for any tenant and globally — with consent captured and retention enforced.


7. Phase 6 — Navigability, production-readiness, and smoke testing

Make it user-ready and prod-ready. The product currently opens on a near-empty admin shell; fix that.

Navigability & UX


The product opens on a meaningful dashboard (active courses, QA scores needing attention, next accreditation deadline, recent activity) — not an empty shell. Re-center the dashboard on the customer's job-to-be-done, not the internal pipeline.
Consistent global navigation, breadcrumbs, no orphan pages or dead ends.
Empty, loading, and error states everywhere. Seed/demo data so a fresh tenant is never staring at a blank screen.
Mobile-responsive.


Production-readiness


Environment config + secrets management (no secrets in code), input validation (Zod) on every endpoint, rate limiting on auth and expensive routes, a health-check endpoint, structured error monitoring, and DB migration + backup discipline. Provide deploy configuration.


Smoke tests (automated) for every critical journey:


Sign up → trial start → land on dashboard.
Create course → author objective → see live measurability/Bloom feedback.
Run server-side QA evaluate → persisted scored report appears.
Generate CCNE evidence packet → download PDF and .docx.
Hit gated feature → Stripe checkout → land on paid plan.
Apply white-label branding → branding renders in UI and in export.
Impersonation start/stop → audit log entry written.
Analytics event fires and surfaces in the founder console.


Also deliver a manual QA checklist mirroring these journeys for human verification before release.


8. Definition of done (the whole build)


A new user can self-serve from signup to paid plan with no human in the loop.
The QA engine runs server-side on real customer data and persists scored reports; the demo and the product share one engine.
A tenant-branded, CCNE-aligned evidence packet exports in one click.
Billing works end to end on a value-based metric (active courses).
White-label branding renders across UI, exports, and emails.
The founder console answers who/what/when/where/how-long and what's-working-vs-not, per-tenant and globally, with consent and retention in place.
All critical journeys pass automated smoke tests; multi-tenant isolation and impersonation safety tests remain green.
The product opens on a meaningful dashboard, has no dead ends, and handles empty/loading/error states throughout.
